## BLS PROCEDURES

### ADULT (HR less than 40 bpm)

Administer oxygen as indicated

Supine position as tolerated

---

### PEDIATRIC (HR less than 60 bpm)

Administer oxygen as indicated

Assist ventilations if needed

If significant ALOC, initiate CPR

---

## ALS STANDING ORDERS

### ADULT

**IV/IO access**

**Obtain 12-lead ECG**

**Atropine**
- IV/IO – 1 mg

If initial Atropine is transiently effective, or patient remains bradycardic without hemodynamic compromise.
- May repeat Atropine 1 mg IV/IO q 5 min to a total max dose of 3 mg.

**Transcutaneous Pacing (TCP)**
- Should be initiated only if patient has signs of hypoperfusion
- Should be started immediately for 3º heart blocks and 2º Type 2 (Mobitz II) heart blocks
- If pain is present during TCP
  - Pain Control– per policy 705.19

### If patient remains hypotensive (SBP less than 90mmHg)

**Epinephrine 10 mcg/mL**
- 1 mL (10 mcg) q 2 minutes, slow IV/IO push
- Titrate to SBP ≥ 90 mm/Hg

### When patient presents or becomes hypotensive without signs of heart failure.

**Normal Saline**
- 500 mL IV/IO bolus
- May repeat x 1 for total of 1,000 mL

### For suspected hyperkalemia

**Calcium Chloride**
- IV/IO – 1 g
  - Withhold if suspected digitalis toxicity

**Sodium Bicarbonate**
- IV/IO – 1 mEq/kg
- Repeat 0.5 mEq/kg x 2 q 5 min

**If CPR indicated, initiate CAM and reference appropriate cardiac arrest treatment protocol**

---

### PEDIATRIC

**IV/IO access**
- IV/IO access only if patient in extremis

**Epinephrine 10 mcg/mL**
- 0.1 mL/kg (1 mcg/kg) q 2 minutes, slow IV/IO push
- Max single dose of 1 mL or 10 mcg
- Titrate to SBP of greater than or equal to 80 mm/Hg

---

## BASE HOSPITAL ORDERS ONLY

**Atropine**
- IV/IO – 0.02 mg/kg
  - Minimum dose – 0.1 mg

Consult with ED Physician for further treatment measure

---

## ADDITIONAL INFORMATION

• Bradycardia does not require treatment unless signs and symptoms are present (chest pain, altered level of consciousness, abnormal skin signs, profound weakness, shortness of breath or low BP)

• Refer to VCEMS Policy 735 for additional information on preparing push dose epinephrine solution.

## SEPsis Alert Criteria

**When infection is suspected and two or more physiologic criteria are met, notify the receiving facility of a 'Sepsis Alert'**

---

## SUSPECTED INFECTION CRITERIA

**Common to Both Adult and Pediatric**

1 or more indicators of infection present, including, but not limited to, the examples below:

- Pneumonia
- Cellulitis
- UTI
- Abdominal Pain
- Wound Infection
- Current Antibiotics
- Immunocompromised

---

## PHYSIOLOGIC CRITERIA

### ADULT - Physiologic Criteria

**2 or more physiologic criteria acutely present:**

- **Perfusion:** SBP < 90 mmHg, HR Sustained > 90
- **Respiratory Rate** > 20
- **Temperature** > 100.4 or hot to the touch
- **EtCO2** Sustained < 25 mmHg

---

### PEDIATRIC - Physiologic Criteria

**2 or more physiologic criteria acutely present:**

- **Perfusion:** SBP < Minimum in Handtevy, HR Sustained above or near Handtevy maximum, Delayed capillary refill or skin signs consistent with distributive shock.
- **Respiratory Rate** above or near Handtevy maximum.
- **Temperature** > 100.4 or hot to the touch
- **EtCO2** Sustained < 25 mmHg

---

## BLS PROCEDURES

Administer Oxygen as indicated

---

## ALS STANDING ORDERS

### ADULT - Sepsis Suspected

**IV/IO Access**

**Normal Saline**
- IV/IO Bolus - 1 Liter

**Suspected Shock**

**Epinephrine 10 mcg/mL**
- 1 mL (10 mcg) q 2 minutes, slow IV/IO push
- Titrate to SBP of greater than or equal to 90 mm/Hg

---

### PEDIATRIC - Sepsis Suspected

**IV/IO Access**

**Normal Saline**
- IV/IO Bolus - 20 mL/kg

**Suspected Shock**

**Epinephrine 10 mcg/mL**
- 0.1 mL/kg (1 mcg/kg) q 2 minutes, slow IV/IO push
- Max single dose of 1 mL or 10 mcg
- Titrate to SBP of greater than or equal to 80 mm/Hg

---

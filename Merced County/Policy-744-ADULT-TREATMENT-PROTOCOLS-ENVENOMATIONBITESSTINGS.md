DEFINITION May present as a localized reaction with redness, pain and swelling at the site or a systemic reaction such as itchiness, flushing of skin, rash, urticaria, tachycardia, and anaphylaxis. Insects Bites and Stings A. Assess wound and remove stinger by scraping with an edge of a stiff object such as a credit card edge. B. May use localized ice pack for pain and swelling. Do not apply ice directly to the skin. Black Widow Spider Bites A. Localized signs and symptoms include immediate pain, redness, and swelling at the site. B. Systemic signs and symptoms may include progressive muscle spasms, severe abdominal pain, nausea and vomiting, sweating, seizures, paralysis, hypertension, and/or diminished level of consciousness. Snakebites A. DO NOT TRANSPORT a live snake, even if you think it is dead, do not touch. Pictures of snake if possible, follow employer guidelines. B. Do not apply ice. C. Do not incise or attempt to suck the venom from the wound. D. Do not apply constricting band or tourniquet. If constricting band or tourniquet has been applied, prior to arrival, do not release. E. Pain, edema, and discoloration may be present. F. Systemic signs and symptoms may be present and could include: 1. Nausea/Vomiting, weakness, diaphoresis, chills, and/or dizziness. Tingling, numbness, and fasciculations (spasms) of face. II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate, goal is to maintain SpO2 at least 94%, secure airway and assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vitals frequently, every 5 minutes if hypotensive. BLOOD SUGAR Test glucose and treat as appropriate if blood sugar is < 60 mg/dl. CHECK TEMPERATURE Assess temperature and keep patients warm. NOTHING BY MOUTH Do not give patients anything to eat or drink. IDENTIFY WOUND Note location of the wound and identify extent of redness or swelling mark it; mark affected area and track swelling every fifteen (15) minutes. IMMOBILIZE/ Immobilize the affected extremity; remove distal jewelry; assess circulation, CIRCULATION sensation, and motor function before and after immobilization. III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat arrhythmias as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS Establish IV/IO rate as appropriate. If Systolic BP is less than 90 mmHg administer 500 ml bolus and repeat as indicated or until Systolic BP is greater than 90 mmHg. PAIN MANAGEMENT Treat pain as appropriate refer to EMS Policy \#718 Adult Treatment Protocols - Pain Management Protocol. BLACK WIDOW If muscle spasms are severe; administer Midazolam 2 mg IV/IO push. Titrate SPIDER BITE in 1 mg increments to control muscle spasms with a maximum dose 5 mg IV/IO. Administer 5 mg IN or IM if no immediate IV/IO access available; may repeat once after 10 minutes if spasms continue. IV. Special Considerations Monitor the patient for signs of anaphylaxis, especially from insect stings. Refer to EMS Policy \#726 Adult Treatment Protocols - Allergic Reaction Protocol.

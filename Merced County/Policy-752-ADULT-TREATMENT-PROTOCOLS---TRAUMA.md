# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 752 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: ADULT TREATMENT PROTOCOLS - TRAUMA Effective Date 7/2023 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 07/2022 Review Date: 07/2025 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. DEFINITION To properly treat patients that suffer from traumatic injuries, including blunt force, penetrating injuries, lacerations, and other forms of traumatic injuries. Patients who suffer from severe traumatic injuries need evaluation and transport to appropriate Trauma Centers. Patients who meet Merced County EMS Agency Trauma Triage Criteria should be transported to a trauma center unless the patient has potential airway compromise. Trauma patients with potential airway problems that cannot be resolved must be transported to the closest hospital, refer to EMS Policy \#402 Patient Destination. Scene time for patients who meet trauma triage criteria should be kept at 10 minutes or less. If the scene time exceeds 15 minutes, the reason for the extended scene delay (waiting for air transport, extended extrication, multiple patients, etc.) must be documented on the Patient Care Report. II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN Administer as appropriate; goal is to maintain SpO₂ at least 94%. Assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vitals; treat patient as appropriate. ASSESS NEED FOR Refer to EMS Policy \#750 Spinal Motion Restriction for proper application SPINAL MOTION of SMR. RESTRICTION (SMR) BLEEDING CONTROL Stop active bleeding with direct pressure, hemostatic dressings, and/or if necessary, apply a tourniquet. SPLINT Immobilize or splint as needed, consider splinting while transporting. TEMPERATURE Assess temperature and keep patient warm to prevent hypothermia. TRANSPORT Patients who meet trauma triage criteria need transport to a trauma center as soon as possible. ALS TREATMENT ASSESS FOR Decompress the affective side as needed, mid-axillary primary site, midTENSION clavicular secondary site. PNUEMOTHORAX MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. IV/IO ACCESS Patients who meet trauma triage criteria, two (2) large bore IV/IO access. Only give fluid bolus if systolic blood pressure is less than 90 mmHg or 100 mmHg for patients greater than 65 years of age, refer to EMS Policy \#758 Adult Treatment Protocols - TXA/Bleeding Control for possible use of TXA if indicated. CAPNOGRAPHY Utilize wave form capnography for patients who meet trauma triage criteria. PAIN CONTROL Refer to EMS Policy \#718 Adult Treatment Protocols - Pain Management, for appropriate pain control. IV. SPECIFIC INJURY TREATMENTS AMPUTATIONS - If partial amputation, splint in anatomical position. Place complete amputated parts in a sealed clean and dry container or bag. Place container or bag on ice if possible. EVISCERATING TRAUMA - Cover eviscerated organs with saline-soaked gauze. Do not attempt to replace organs in the abdominal cavity. EXTREMITY TRAUMA - Apply appropriate splint/immobilization device, check neurovascular status before and after splinting/immobilizing. FLAILED CHEST - Consider laying patient on the affective side to minimize chest wall movement, monitor for tension pneumothorax. IMPALED OBJECT - Stabilize and leave impaled object in place. If impaled object causes airway compromise or interferes with CPR, object can be removed. OPEN CHEST WOUND - Apply commercial vented chest seal or 3-sided occlusive dressing over wound. Watch for signs of developing tension pneumothorax and treat as appropriate.

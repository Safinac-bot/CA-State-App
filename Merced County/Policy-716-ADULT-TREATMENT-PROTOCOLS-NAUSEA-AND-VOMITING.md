# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 716 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: ADULT TREATMENT PROTOCOLS – NAUSEA AND VOMITING Effective Date 10/2024 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 01/2017 Review Date: 10/2026 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. GENERAL INDICATIONS To alleviate nausea and vomiting from a variety of causes including but not limited to gastroenteritis, motion sickness, appendicitis. Ondansetron may be administered prior to transport to prevent nausea/vomiting. May be administered to prevent nausea/vomiting from pain medication administration. May administer diphenhydramine as an alternative if allergic to ondansetron. II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate. Keep SpO2 at least 94%, assist ventilations as necessary. Be prepared to suction airway if necessary. VITALS Assess vital signs. BLOOD SUGAR Test blood sugar if indicated. CHECK III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS Rate as appropriate; if severe vomiting or signs of dehydration administer if patient has a systolic BP < 90. Administer 250 ml fluid boluses to systolic BP > 90. Reassess patient after each bolus. ONDANSETRON Administer 4 mg ODT, IV/IO, IM. Attempt to administer ODT as first route of choice if possible; may administer every 10 minutes as necessary to a max dose of 12 mg total. Do not use ODT in patients with a GCS < 14. DIPHENHYDRAMINE Administer 50 mg PO/IM, or 25 mg IV/IO may be administered if patient is allergic to Ondansetron. May repeat once to a max dose of 50 mg total. SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS • Syncope may occur when administering IV ondansetron, so administer slowly over 60 seconds. • Ondansetron is safe for patients who are pregnant or breast feeding. • For prolonged transport time consider Diphenhydramine for motion sickness.

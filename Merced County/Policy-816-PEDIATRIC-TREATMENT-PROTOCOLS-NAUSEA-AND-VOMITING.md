# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 816 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS – NAUSEA AND VOMITING Effective Date 01/2024 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 01/2017 Review Date: 01/2026 PAGE 1 of 1 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director S T R I V I N G F O R E X C E L L E N C E I. GENERAL INDICATIONS To alleviate nausea and vomiting from a variety of causes including but not limited to gastroenteritis, motion sickness, appendicitis. May be given prior to transport to prevent nausea/vomiting. May be given to prevent nausea/vomiting from pain medication administration. May use diphenhydramine as an alternative if allergic to ondansetron. II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate. Keep SpO₂ at least 94%, assist ventilations as necessary. Be prepared to suction airway if necessary. VITALS Assess vitals. BLOOD SUGAR Test blood sugar if indicated. CHECK III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS Rate as appropriate; if severe vomiting or signs of dehydration administer 20 ml/kg fluid bolus and reassess patient; repeat as necessary. ONDANSETRON Administer 2-4 mg ODT, IV/IO, IM. Try to administer ODT as first route of choice if possible; may administer every 10 minutes as necessary to max dose of 12 mg total. DIPHENHYDRAMINE Administer 1 mg/kg PO, IM or IV/IO up to a max of 25 mg. An alternative if allergic to ondansetron. Refer to length-based assessment tape or application for drug doses and proper equipment sizes.

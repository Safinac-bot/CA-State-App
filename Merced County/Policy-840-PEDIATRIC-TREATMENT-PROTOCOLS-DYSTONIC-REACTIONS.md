# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 840 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS – DYSTONIC REACTIONS Effective Date 04/2024 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 11/2018 Review Date: 04/2026 PAGE 1 of 1 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. DEFINITION Phenothiazines are prescribed for their antiemetic and anti-cholinergic properties. Common phenothiazines include: • Chlorpromazine (Thorazine), • Metoclopramide (Reglan), • Prochlorperazine (Compazine) and • Promethazine (Phenergan). Non-phenothiazine medication that can cause dystonic reactions include: • Haloperidol (Haldol). Symptoms might include restlessness; muscle spasms of the neck; jaw and back; movement of eyeballs (oculogyric crisis); frightened; facial grimace; protruding tongue, back arching (opisthotonus). II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate. Keep SpO2 at least 94%, assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vitals signs. BLOOD SUGAR Test blood glucose, if less than 80 mg/dl refer to EMS Policy \#824 Pediatric Treatment Protocols – Altered Loss of Consciousness (ALOC) Policy. III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS IV/IO TKO DIPHENHYDRAMINE Administer 1 mg/kg IV/IO or IM; maximum single dose of 25 mg. May repeat once after 5 minutes if no response. Refer to length-based assessment tape or application for drug doses and proper equipment sizes.

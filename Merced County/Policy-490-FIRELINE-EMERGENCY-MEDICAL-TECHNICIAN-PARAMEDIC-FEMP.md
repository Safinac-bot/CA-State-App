# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 490 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: FIRELINE EMERGENCY MEDICAL TECHNICIAN – PARAMEDIC (FEMP) Effective Date: 05/2025 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, Section 1797.204 and 1797.220-226 California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Sections 100165 and 100167 FIRESCOPE - Fireline Emergency Medical Technician – Paramedic (FEMP ICS 702) Initial Date: 05/2018 Review Date: 05/2028 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. PURPOSE To establish policy allowing Paramedics to function as Fireline Paramedics and provide ALS care outside the Local EMS Agency jurisdiction when requested through the Statewide Fire and Rescue Mutual Aid System. II. POLICY A Paramedic currently licensed by the State of California and accredited by the Merced County EMS Agency may be authorized to provide ALS care while operating in the capacity of a Fireline Paramedic upon successfully meeting all requirements of this policy. III. DEFINITIONS A. Fireline Paramedics is a state licensed and locally accredited Paramedic who is currently employed by an approved Fireline Paramedic Provider and meets all requirements and standards established by the California Incident Command Certification System and FIRESCOPE (ICS 702). B. Fireline Paramedic Providers is a fire agency approved by the Merced County EMS Agency to provide Fireline Paramedic services on-scene of an incident during a mutual aid response. IV. PROCEDURE Fire agencies seeking authorization to become a Fireline Paramedic provider shall submit a written request to the Merced County EMS Agency. Participating Fireline Paramedic providers shall be required to provide an operational plan updated once every two years or upon proposing any significant changes. The operational plan shall address the following components: A. Continue Education/Skills Maintenance Plan for Fireline Paramedic personnel. B. Name, license number and California Incident Command Certification System (CICCS) qualifications of Fireline Paramedic personnel. C. Medication restock and controlled substance security plan. Subject: FIRELINE EMERGENCY MEDICAL TECHNICIAN – PARAMEDIC (FEMP) Policy Number 490 Page 2 of 2 STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE D. QA/QI program plan. E. Additional information as required by the Merced County EMS Agency. F. During mutual aid response into another jurisdiction, a Fireline Paramedic may provide ALS interventions that are Standing Orders only. At no time shall a Fireline Paramedic perform any procedure or intervention identified as Base Hospital or Physician Orders. G. Fireline Paramedic personnel shall only provide ALS interventions to personnel suffering from a medical condition or traumatic injury who are assigned to the mutual aid incident. H. The Fireline Paramedic must be on duty and employed by an approved Fireline Paramedic Provider. I. Patient Care Reports records shall be maintained in accordance with the Merced County EMS Agency Policies and Procedures. J. The Fireline Paramedic shall provide a brief written Patient Care Report (i.e., Ambulance Report Form) to ambulance personnel, when possible, before initiation of patient transport. K. The participating Fireline Paramedic provider shall provide incident reports, related data, or program evaluations to the Merced County EMS Agency upon request. V. EQUIPMENT The Fireline Paramedic shall carry a minimum supply of medications and equipment consistent with the FIRESCOPE ICS 702 Appendix B Fireline ALS Pack Inventory.

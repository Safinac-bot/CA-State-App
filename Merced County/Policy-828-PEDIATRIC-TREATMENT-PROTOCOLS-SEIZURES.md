# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 828 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS – SEIZURES Effective Date 01/2024 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 10/2011 Review Date: 01/2026 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. DEFINITION All seizures from any cause (epilepsy, hypoglycemia, infection, intoxication, poisoning, or eclampsia) are managed the same. Remember to provide supportive care and to check for life threatening causes. Reassess the patient and watch for recurrent seizure activity. Status Seizures – Any actively seizing patient who has been seizing for more than 10 minutes, or an actively seizing patient with recurrent seizures and no reawakening in between seizures. Febrile Seizures – First time seizures in pediatric patients can be febrile in nature. Assess the temperature of the patient, if available, and address elevated temperatures with cooling measures (e.g., remove clothing, unwrap blankets, and use cold packs if necessary). II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate, goal is to maintain SpO2 at least 94%, secure airway and assist ventilations as necessary. POSITION If not contraindicated by injuries, place patient in left lateral decubitus position. VITALS Assess vitals, monitor airway and ventilatory effort and assist ventilations as needed. BLOOD SUGAR Test glucose and treat as appropriate if blood sugar is < 80 mg/dl. Refer to CHECK EMS Policy \#824 Pediatric Treatment Protocols – Altered Level of Consciences (ALOC) or Syncope III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS IV/IO TKO. MIDAZOLAM Administer 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO (max dose 5 mg) or 0.2 mg/kg IN/IM (max dose 5 mg) if no immediate IV/IO access. May repeat once after 10 minutes if seizures continue. Subject: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS – SEIZURES Policy Number 828 Page 2 of 2 STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE IV. SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS Manage airway with aggressive BLS techniques (suction, airway adjuncts, BVM) if necessary, providers may use approved supraglottic airway to secure airway. Refer to length-based assessment tape or application for drug doses and proper equipment sizes.

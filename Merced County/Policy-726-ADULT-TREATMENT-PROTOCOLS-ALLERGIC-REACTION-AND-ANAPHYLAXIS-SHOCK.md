DEFINITION ALLERGIC REACTION – Hives, rash, flushing, swelling to allergen site with normal vital signs, a local reaction to an allergen. Remember that allergic reactions can escalate into anaphylaxis; reassess often and be prepared to treat for anaphylaxis. ANAPHYLAXIS – Wheezing, stridor, oral/facial edema, hypotension, severe respiratory distress, ALOC, chest tightness. A systemic response to an allergen involving 2 or more organ systems or any deterioration of vital signs. EMT Optional Scope (EMT-OS) – Accreditation for EMTs to practice optional skills shall be limited to those whose EMT certificate is active and are employed within the jurisdiction of the LEMSA by an employer who is part of the organized EMS system. II. BLS TREATMENT REMOVE If feasible, scrape out bee stinger and apply cold pack. ALLERGEN OXYGEN As appropriate, goal is to maintain SpO2 at least 94%, assist ventilations as necessary. PATIENT’S Assist patient with Albuterol/Ventolin MDI if available. MEDICATION VITALS Assess vital signs and assess patient for signs of anaphylaxis. May repeat after 5-10 minutes if no improvement. Maximum of 3 doses. III. ALS TREATMENT ALLERGIC REACTION MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS IV/IO as appropriate, TKO. DIPHENHYDRAMINE Administer 25 mg IV/IM or 50 mg PO if patient can swallow for patients who present with hives, rash, or swelling and weight is greater than or equal to 30 kg/66 lbs. ANAPHYLAXIS EPINEPHRINE Administer 0.01 mg/kg IM of 1:1,000 (max dose 0.5 mg). May repeat after 3-5 minutes if no improvement. Consider using IV epinephrine after a second dose if no improvement. VASCULAR ACCESS IV/IO as appropriate; establish two (2) 14 or 16 gauge, if patient has a systolic BP less than 90 mmHg administer 250 ml fluid boluses to a systolic BP greater than 90 mmHg. Reassess patient after each bolus. DIPHENHYDRAMINE Administer 50 mg IV/IO. May administer 50 mg IM if no immediate IV/IO access is available. ALBUTEROL Administer 5.0 mg (2 x 2.5 mg doses) via nebulizer for respiratory distress with wheezes. Repeat as necessary. EPINEPHERINE Administer 0.1 mg (1 ml) of 1:10,000 slow IV/IO push if systolic BP is less than 90 and/or the patient has severe respiratory distress. May repeat every 2-3 minutes to systolic BP greater than 90 or improved respiratory effort.

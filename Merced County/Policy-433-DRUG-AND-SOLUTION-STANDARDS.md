# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 433 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: DRUG AND SOLUTION STANDARDS Effective Date 07/2024 Authority: California Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, Sections 1797.220 and 1798(a) Initial Date: 12/2020 Review Date: 07/2026 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. PURPOSE To establish consistent minimum Drug and Solution inventory standards for Basic Life Support, Advanced Life Support, ALS First Responders, and Special Event units or vehicles within Merced County. II. POLICY Authorized EMS providers in Merced County, including special event units or vehicles, shall maintain a Drug and Solution inventory of all items within these standards established by the Merced County EMS Agency. III. PROCEDURE A. The provider agency shall ensure that each authorized drug and solution inventory is maintained and stored consistent with Federal, State and Local EMS Policy. B. Drug and solution inventory, which have been used, broken, near their expiration shall be replaced as soon as possible. C. Drug and solution inventories shall be checked (inventoried) each day. This shall include stored inventories not currently in use (must comply with Drug Enforcement Agency (DEA) regulations). D. Any deficiencies of the drug and solution inventory shall require that the ALS units be removedfrom service until the deficiencies are corrected. E. A complete drug and solution inventory (except IV solutions) shall accompanythe patient in the transporting vehicle. F. Annual and random inspections by EMS Agency personnel will be made to verify that ALS units are maintained in compliance with requirements.

# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 830 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS - HYPERGLYCEMIA Effective Date 10/2024 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 11/2018 Review Date: 10/2026 PAGE 1 of 1 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. DEFINITION Elevated blood glucose levels (Hyperglycemia) can be a medical emergency. • High glucose levels in Type 1 diabetics can cause diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA). • High glucose in Type 2 diabetics can cause hyperosmolar hyperglycemic non-ketoacidosis (HHNK). Both DKA and HHNK are characterized by: Thirst and increased urination, confusion, dehydration, deep and rapid respirations, nausea, vomiting, fruity odor on breath, missed insulin dose, or noncompliant with diabetic medications. Determine if patient has been diagnosed with diabetes previously. II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate, goal to maintain SpO2 at least 94%. Assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vital signs. BLOOD SUGAR Test blood glucose. III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. CAPNOGRAPHY Utilize waveform capnography if blood sugar is 200 mg/dl or greater. EtCO2 readings of 25 mmHg or less is suggestive of acidosis (DKA). VASCULAR ACCESS Establish IV/IO and administer fluid bolus of 20 ml/kg, if blood sugar is 200 mg/dl or greater and EtCO2 of 25 mmHg or less. Do not establish an IO if the patient has a GCS of 14 or greater. MAKE BASE HOSPITAL CONTACT FOR REPEAT FLUID BOLUSES. Refer to length-based assessment tape or application for drug doses and proper equipment sizes.

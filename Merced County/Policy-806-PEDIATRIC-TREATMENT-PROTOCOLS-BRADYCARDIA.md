# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 806 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS – BRADYCARDIA Effective Date 10/2024 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 07/2022 Review Date: 10/2026 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. DEFINITION Symptomatic bradycardia is defined by the following: • Heart rate below 60 beats/minute • Systolic Blood Pressure – hypotensive per length-based assessment tape • Associated signs and symptoms including ALOC, signs of shock, chest pain, acute pulmonary edema, syncope, extreme weakness • The following medications may cause symptomatic bradycardia: beta blocker, calcium channel blockers, digoxin, organophosphates and amiodarone. II. BLS TREATMENT CPR If heart rate is less than 60 beats/minute with severe signs of poor perfusion and shock, begin CPR – Refer to EMS Policy \#802 Pediatric Treatment Protocols - Cardiac Arrest. OXYGEN As appropriate, goal is to maintain SpO2 at least 94%, secure airway and assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vital signs frequently, every 5 minutes if hypotensive. BLOOD SUGAR Test glucose and treat as appropriate per EMS Policy \#824 Pediatric CHECK Treatment Protocols - Altered Level of Consciences (ALOC) Or Syncope. III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat arrhythmias as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS If patient is hypotensive, establish two (2) large bore IV/IO’s and administer 20 ml/kg bolus. Reassess patient after bolus. Repeat, if necessary, at 10 ml/kg bolus up to a total of 40 ml/kg max has been administered. EPINEPHRINE Administer 1:10,000 of 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO repeat every 3 minutes as needed. ATROPINE If no response to EPINEPHERINE, consider administering 0.02 mg/kg IV/IO, with a 0.1 mg minimum dose to a 0.5 mg maximum dose. TRANSCUTANEOUS BASE HOSPITAL PHYSICIAN ORDER ONLY PACING (TCP): • Set rate at 80 beats/minute • Initiate TCP and increase current in 10 mA increments looking for mechanical capture with pulses are noted • Once mechanical capture is confirmed, increase current output up to 5-10 mA • Patients that need sedation or pain control to tolerate TCP consider using one of the following: o Sedation  Midazolam 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO o Pain Management  Morphine 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO or  Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg IV/IO Refer to length-based assessment tape or application for drug doses and proper equipment sizes.

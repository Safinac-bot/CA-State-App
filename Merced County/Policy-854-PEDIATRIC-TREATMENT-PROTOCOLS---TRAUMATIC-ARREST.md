# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 854 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS - TRAUMATIC ARREST Effective Date 09/2023 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 07/2022 Review Date: 09/2025 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. DEFINITION Patients who are in cardiac arrest from severe traumatic injuries need immediate treatment and reversal of the cause of the cardiac arrest, (e.g., tension pneumothorax, severe hemorrhage, hypoxia, etc.). All reversible conditions must be addressed immediately. If the transport time to an emergency department is less than 10 minutes, consider rapid transport if the patient would benefit. Do not transport patients in traumatic arrest via air ambulance. II. BLS TREATMENT ASSESS NEED FOR Refer to EMS Policy \#750 Spinal Motion Restriction for proper application SPINAL MOTION of SMR. RESTRICTION (SMR) CPR Begin chest compressions following EMS Policy \#802 Pediatric Treatment Protocols - Cardiac Arrest. AIRWAY Utilize simplest airway adjuncts with bag-valve-mask (BVM) to provide adequate ventilations. If advanced airway is placed, place EtCO₂ to monitor capnography for proper airway. BLEEDING CONTROL Stop active bleeding with direct pressure, hemostatic dressings, and/or if necessary, apply a tourniquet. III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. NEEDLE If patient suffers from blunt force trauma, perform bilateral needle THORACOSTOMY decompression. VASCULAR ACCESS Establish IV/IO and administer 20 ml/kg bolus. Reassess patient after bolus. Repeat if necessary and administer 10 ml/kg bolus to a MAX of 40 ml/kg. CARDIAC Refer to EMS Policy \#802 Pediatric Treatment Protocols - Cardiac Arrest, MEDICATIONS treat patient accordingly. DETERMINATION OF DEATH If patient is in asystole or idioventricular rhythm less than 20 beats/minute, after two (2) minutes of CPR, if there is no change in patient condition, terminate CPR and cease efforts. If patient is in Ventricular Fibrillation (V-fib) or Pulseless Electrical Activity (PEA) greater than 20 beats/minute, work patient up to 10 minutes looking for any reversable causes, if there is no change in patient condition, terminate CPR and cease efforts. If at any time the patient achieves return of spontaneous circulation (ROSC), provide immediate transport to the closest emergency department. If the patient loses ROSC prior to transport, begin CPR until the patient achieves ROSC or 10 minutes of elapsed time with no response.

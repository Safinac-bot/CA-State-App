# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 512 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: TRAUMA SYSTEM ORGANIZATION AND MANAGEMENT Effective Date 04/2024 Authority: California Health and Safety Code, Sections 1797.222, 1797.220 California Code of Regulations, Section 100255 Initial Date: 05/2005 Review Date: 04/2026 PAGE 1 of 3 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. PURPOSE The purpose of the Trauma System Organization and Management Policy is to establish the roles and responsibilities of the Merced County EMS Agency (MCEMSA) in the planning, implementation, and ongoing evaluation of an inclusive trauma system. II. POLICY Merced County EMS Agency shall be responsible for the planning, implementation, evaluation, and revision of the Merced County Trauma System. Currently the designated Trauma Centers for Adult Trauma in Merced County are in Stanislaus or Fresno County depending on the closest and most appropriate destination. Pediatric Trauma is designated for transport to Madera County or the closest and most appropriate destination. Burn patients shall go to the appropriate Burn Center. It shall also be the responsibility of the MCEMSA to coordinate and collaborate with the Stanislaus EMS Agency and Central California EMS Agency in the on-going oversight of those Level I and II Trauma Centers and the related activities of our respective trauma systems. III. DEFINITIONS Trauma Audit Committee (TAC) - Shall mean a quality improvement body made up of participant hospital ED physicians, surgeons and subject matter experts organized to review and provide the EMS agencies and participant hospitals with recommendations regarding the quality of services provided. IV. PROCEDURE A. As the lead agency for the Merced County EMS System, MCEMSA is responsible for planning, implementing, and managing the trauma care system. These responsibilities include: 1. Assessing trauma system needs. 2. Developing the system design, including the number of trauma centers. 3. Assigning roles to system participants, including the designation of local trauma center(s) and recognition of remote trauma centers, as necessary. Coordinating system activities with designated trauma center and other system participants, and with neighboring EMS systems on coordination and mutual aid issues. 5. Coordination for the collection of trauma data, including a trauma registry at the trauma centers, trauma data collection from participating non-trauma centers, and pre-hospital data collection. 6. Monitoring of the system to determine compliance with appropriate state laws and regulations, local EMS agency policies and procedures, and contracts, and taking corrective action as needed. 7. Evaluating the impact of the system and revising the system design as needed. B. # 17 EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 518 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: TRAUMA FACILITY DESIGNATION Effective Date 01/2025 Authority: California Health and Safety Code, Section 1798.160, 1798.162, 1798.163, 1798.164, 1798.165, and 1798.170 California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Section 100255, 100257, 100259, 100260, and 100265 Initial Date: 11/1998 Review Date: 01/2027 PAGE 1 of 3 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. PURPOSE To establish the process and criteria by which the Merced County EMS Agency designates a Trauma Center(s) for hospitals within Merced County when applying for designation as a specific trauma facility (e.g. Level II, Level III, Level IV). II. POLICY Hospitals in Merced County shall submit a letter and follow the required procedure as provided in this policy. Hospitals interested in obtaining or changing trauma center designation must provide a letter of intent to the Merced County EMS Agency to seek trauma center designation specifying the level of designation desired. III. DEFINITIONS A. Trauma case means any injured person who has been evaluated by prehospital personnel according to policies and procedures established by the local EMS agency pursuant to Section 1798.163 and who has been found to require transportation to a trauma facility. B. Trauma facility means a health facility, as defined by regulation, which is capable of treating one or more types of potentially seriously injured persons and which has been designated as part of the regional trauma care system by the local EMS agency. A facility may be a trauma facility for one or more services, as designated by the local EMS agency. C. Trauma care system means an arrangement under which trauma cases are transported to, and treated by, the appropriate trauma facility. IV. PROCEDURE A. Designation 1. A consideration of a Trauma Center designation following the satisfactory review of a written documentation and a site survey when deemed necessary by the Merced County EMS Agency. Subject: TRAUMA FACILITY APPLICATION PROCESS Policy Number 518 Page 2 of 3 STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE 2. Application Documents: a. Written Letter of intent from Trauma Program Medical Director. b. Request for trauma center level of designation. c. Fees (non-refundable), including those for the application, designation and redesignation, monitoring and evaluation. 3. Designation Review and Approval Process: a. Merced County EMS Agency shall provide the requesting trauma center with the Trauma Application within 14 days of receiving the Letter of Intent. b. Merced County EMS Agency shall notify the requesting trauma center, in writing, within 14 days of their submission if there are any missing components within their application. c. Merced County EMS Agency shall respond, in writing, to the requesting trauma center of their approval or denial within 30 days after receiving all required documentation. d. Trauma center designation/redesignation process to include a written agreement between the local EMS agency and the trauma center. 4. Redesignation a. Trauma centers shall be reviewed every three years unless multiple deficiencies are found, which would then require a yearly review until they are resolved. Merced County EMS Agency may use the American College of Surgeons verification and reverification as the review. b. Merced County EMS Agency may suspend or revoke the approval of a trauma center at any time for failure to comply with any critical applicable policies, procedures, or regulations. c. A trauma center may be re-designated following a satisfactory Merced County EMS Agency or American College of Surgeons review. d. Trauma centers shall receive notification of the evaluation from Merced County EMS Agency or American College of Surgeons. e. Trauma centers will provide in writing the American College of Surgeons findings regarding program compliance. f. Trauma centers shall notify the Merced County EMS Agency by telephone, followed by a letter or email within 48 hours of changes in program compliance or performance. Trauma centers shall submit a written 180 calendar-day notice to the Merced County EMS Agency prior to the discontinuation of SRC services.

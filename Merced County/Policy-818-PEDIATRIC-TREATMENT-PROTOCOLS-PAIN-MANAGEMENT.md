DEFINITION Pain is a subjective unpleasant sensory and emotional experience associated with actual or potential tissue damage. Any patient with a complaint of moderate or significant pain including but not limited to burn patients, frostbite, bites and envenomation, crush injuries, extremity injuries, traumatic injuries, abdominal pain, sickle cell crisis, cancer, prolonged extrication, renal colic, etc. May use morphine or fentanyl alone or in combination up to a max of 10 mg morphine equivalents. Morphine Equivalents: 10 mg morphine = 100 mcg fentanyl 5 mg morphine \& 50 mcg fentanyl = 10 mg morphine II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate. Keep SpO2 at least 94%, assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vital signs and check for pulse up to 60 seconds. POSITION Splint injured extremity, ice and elevation as needed to prevent swelling. PSYCHOLOGICAL Reassure patient. SUPPORT III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS Establish IV/IO normal saline preferred, rather than saline lock. CAPNOGRAPHY Utilize wave form capnography for narcotic doses greater than 10 mg morphine equivalent or with ketamine administration. MILD TO MODERATE PAIN – 1 TO 5 ON PAIN SCALE. May Administer One or Both of These Agents. Consider alternate IV/IO site if necessary. KETOROLAC Administer 0.5 mg/kg IM/IN up to 15 mg max dose or slow IV/IO push over 15 seconds SINGLE DOSE ONLY. Acetaminophen and/or Ketorolac may be administered in addition to opioids for patients with severe pain. Ketorolac is the preferred agent for patients with suspected kidney stones or chronic back pain. Do not administer acetaminophen to patients with severe hepatic impairment or active liver disease. Do not administer ketorolac with history of renal disease or kidney transplant; hypotension defined as systolic BP less than 90 mm Hg; history of GI bleeding or ulcers; current anticoagulation therapy or active bleeding; current steroid use; greater than 65 years of age; known allergy or hypersensitivity to NSAIDS (non-steroidal anti-inflammatory medications); history of Asthma; pregnant or high possibility of pregnancy; headaches suspected from acute intracranial bleed. SEVERE PAIN – 5 OR HIGHER ON PAIN SCALE CAPNOGRAPHY Utilize wave form capnography for narcotic doses greater than 5 mg morphine equivalent or with ketamine administration. MORPHINE Administer IV/IO 0.1 mg/kg. May repeat every 5 to 10 minutes ONLY if systolic SULFATE BP is above length-based assessment tape target. Max single dose is 2.5 mg. Max total dose 10 mg. Administer IM 0.1 mg/kg. May repeat once in 10 to 15 minutes ONLY if systolic BP is above length-based assessment tape target. Max single dose is 2.5 mg. If a repeat dose is needed, highly consider IV access. Max total dose 10 mg. FENTANYL Administer IV/IO 1 mcg/kg max dose 25 mcg slow IV push. May repeat every 5 minutes at 0.5 mcg/kg to 1 mcg/kg max dose of 25 mcg ONLY if systolic BP is above length-based assessment tape target. Total max dose of 100 mcg. Administer IM 1 mcg/kg max dose 50 mcg. May repeat every 15 to 20 minutes, ONLY if systolic BP is above length-based assessment tape target; up to a max of 100 mcg. If a repeat dose is needed, highly consider IV/IO access. Administer IN 1.5 mcg/kg 50 mcg single max dose. Administer half (½) dose to each nostril. May repeat once after 10 minutes ONLY if systolic BP is above length-based assessment tape target. NOTE: BASE HOSPITAL CONTACT NEEDED FOR FENTANYL DOSES OVER 100 mcg. Initial vital signs (including weight) and initial pain score and subsequent pain scores (Utilize standard pain scales, i.e., Wong-Baker or numeric scale). 2. GCS: pre, during, and post medication administration. 3. Time medication was given. 4. Total dose of medication. 5. Any adverse or side effects related to medication. MANDATED PAIN SCALE IV. SPECAIL CONSIDERATIONS Administer Fentanyl slowly to prevent Rigid Chest Syndrome. Naloxone may reverse Rigid Chest Syndrome. Ondansetron can be used to prevent or treat nausea associated with narcotic administration, please refer to EMS POLICY \#816 - Pediatric Treatment Protocols - Nausea and Vomiting. Do not utilize with patients who have a GCS less than 14. Use with caution in patients who have sustained a traumatic brain injury and are under the influence of other substances (alcohol, illicit drugs, and sedatives/hypnotics). If a patient shows signs of respiratory depression (shallow respirations and rate less than 8) administer Naloxone 0.5 mg IV/IO, IN, and IM as necessary to increase respiratory rate. Do not use Naloxone for pinpoint pupils only.

# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 824 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS ALTERED LEVEL OF CONSCIOUSNESS (ALOC) OR SYNCOPE Effective Date 09/2023 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 05/2018 Review Date: 09/2025 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. DEFINITION Altered Level of Consciousness (ALOC) or also referred to as altered mental status is characterized by a Glasgow Coma Score of less than 15, confusion, and/or unconsciousness. II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate, goal to maintain SpO2 at least 94%, assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vitals. BLOOD SUGAR Test blood glucose. Record value on Patient Care Report and GCS at time of CHECK fingerstick. ORAL GLUCOSE Administer one (1) unit dose if blood glucose is less than 80 mg/dl with history of diabetes and intact gag reflex (patient able to manage their own secretions). May repeat in 3-5 minutes if altered mental status persists and repeat blood glucose is less than 80 mg/dl. NALOXONE If suspected narcotic intoxication and respiratory depression (rate 8 or less), administer intranasally using mucosal atomizer device (MAD) as follows: • If less than 20 kg, administered 1 mg IN (0.5mg per nasal), • If greater than 20 kg, administered 2 mg IN (1 mg per nasal), or • Administer FULL DOSE of prepackaged Naloxone and spray in one nostril. • May repeat after 3-5 minutes if no improvement or response. III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS TKO. If patient has a low systolic BP or signs of shock, administer fluid bolus 20 ml/kg, and reassess BP. Repeat as necessary. GLUCAGON If blood sugar is less than 80 mg/dl and no IV/IO access administer Glucagon as follows: • If less than 20 kg 0.5 mg (0.5 ml) IM. May repeat after 3-5 minutes if no improvement • If greater than 20 kg 1 mg IM. May repeat after 3-5 minutes if no improvement NALOXONE If suspected narcotic intoxication and respiratory depression (rate 8 or less), administer intranasally using mucosal atomizer device (MAD) as follows: • If less than 20 kg – 1 mg IN (0.5mg per nostril) • If greater than 20 kg – 2 mg IN (1 mg per nostril) Repeat after 3-5 minutes if no response after IN doses as follows: • Administer 0.5 mg IV/IM. • Titrate to increased respiratory effort or until a total of 8 mg of naloxone has been administered. IV. SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS Manage airway with aggressive BLS techniques (suction, airway adjuncts, BVM) if necessary, providers may use approved supraglottic airway to secure airway. Refer to length-based assessment tape or application for drug doses and proper equipment sizes.

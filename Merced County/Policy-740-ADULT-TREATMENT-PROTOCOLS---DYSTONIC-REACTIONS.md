DEFINITION Phenothiazines are prescribed for their antiemetic and anti-cholinergic properties. Common phenothiazines include: • Chlorpromazine (Thorazine), • Metoclopramide (Reglan), • Prochlorperazine (Compazine), and • Promethazine (Phenergan). A non-phenothiazine medication that can cause dystonic reactions is: • Haloperidol (Haldol). Symptoms might include restlessness; muscle spasms of the neck; jaw and back; movement of eyeballs (oculogyric crisis); frightened; facial grimace; protruding tongue, back arching (opisthotonos). II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate, goal is to maintain SpO₂ at least 94%, secure airway and assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vitals. BLOOD SUGAR Test glucose and treat as appropriate. CHECK III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS IV/IO access TKO.

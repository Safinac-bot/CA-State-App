# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 560 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: CRITICAL CARE TRANSPORTATION AMBULANCE AUTHORIZATION Effective Date 10/2024 Authority: California Health and Safety Code Sections 1797.220 and 1797.222 Merced County Code Sections 9.44.010 – 9.44.200 Initial Date: 09/1993 Review Date: 10/2026 PAGE 1 of 3 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. PURPOSE The purpose of this policy shall be to establish the requirements for physician or nurse-staffed interfacility transport units, also known as nurse-staffed Critical Care Transport (CCT) and to establish standards for operation, program approval and licensure of CCT programs based or operating within MercedCounty. II. POLICY All CCT programs based or operating within Merced County shall be licensed by the EMS Agency prior to initiating CCT services originating from within Merced County, and operations shall be restricted as contained herein. III. PROCEDURE A. Program applications submitted for approval shall meet the minimum standards as established by Merced County Code Section 9.44.010 – 9.44-200 and this policy. Critical Care Transport providers shall not be approved for service until such time as being licensed or permitted for service has been executed with the County of Merced. B. Critical Care Transport operations, as delineated in Merced County Code Section 9.44 et. seq., are ALS ambulances and shall be subject to the same operational restrictions as an ALS ambulance operation relative to the application of Merced County ambulance exclusivity authority. C. The CCT provider shall develop a written mission statement that defines the scope of care to be provided by the service. This statement shall also include the criteria establishing which patients are eligible for CCT transfers. D. All patient care resources, including personnel and equipment necessary to the program’s mission must be available in the transport vehicle while transporting a patient. This includes all ALS medications and equipment currently required by the Agency along with additional medication s and equipment as required to fulfill the mission statement and treatment protocols of the CCT provider. E. There shall be an educational program and/or printed information made available to the medical professional community regarding services offered through CCT transport as well as the specific patient criteria. Critical Care Transport providers shall develop and maintain treatment protocols, separate and distinct from the Agency's treatment protocols, which are consistent with the provider's mission statement and scope of services. G. Critical Care Transport providers, licensed to operate within Merced County, shall be promptly available 24 hours a day, seven days a week to respond to requests for service. IV. Critical Care Transport Program Medical Director A. All CCT providers shall have a formal relationship with a CCT Program Medical Director either as an employee of the provider or under a contractual arrangement. B. The Medical Director(s) shall be licensed and authorized to practice medicine in California. He or she must have board certification or substantial experience in emergency or acute critical care and be approved by the EMS Agency Medical Director. Additionally, the EMS Agency reserves the right to allow the CCT Provider to temporarily contract with specialty physicians for consultation and training purposes if no Medical Director with the above qualifications is available. The provider shall submit a copy of these contracts to the Agency upon application for CCT program approval, or whenever a proposed change occurs with the provider’s Medical Director. C. The CCT provider shall submit to the EMS Agency and maintain a record of the Medical Director's educational experience and/or board certifications. The CCT provider shall, if applicable, maintain records of the educational experience and/or board certifications of the specialty physician consultants and make same available for review by theAgency. D. The Medical Director shall be responsible for the following program areas: 1. Development and implementation of the program mission statement and patient treatment protocols. 2. Personnel training program approval. 3. Quality improvement / medical oversight and quality assurance outcome audits are conducted for the CCT program, and all medical personnel associated with the program medical personnel associated with the program. 4. Approval of all CCT provider policies involving patient care, equipment and medications and patient communications. V. Critical Care Transport Personnel Standards A. The CCT provider shall submit proposed requirements for Physician or RN personnel to the EMS Agency Medical Director for review and approval. Minimum CCT personnel configuration shall be as follows: 1. One (1) physician or registered nurse with a minimum of two (2) years of experience in a critical care or emergency department setting. One (1) Merced County accredited paramedic from an ALS approved EMS provider; and 3. One (1) California certified EMT driver. 4. One (1) California certified EMT and one (1) EMT driver can be in lieu of 2 and 3 above. B. The CCT provider, with their Medical Director, shall develop and implement a comprehensive personnel training program. This program will address initial training as well as regular competency evaluation and continuing education. The CQI committee findings shall drive the training program. VI. Quality Improvement A. The CCT provider shall establish and maintain an individual in the position of Clinical Care Supervisor. The provider will establish the minimum education and clinical experience of the Clinical Care Supervisor, along with a detailed job description. This individual shall be responsible for coordinating the quality improvement program for theprovider. B. The CCT provider shall establish a formal continuous quality improvement (CQI) policy and committee, in collaboration with the EMS Agency. This policy shall clearly specify the following, at aminimum: 1. List of quality indicators to be used. 2. Make-up of the CQI committee. 3. Frequency of meetings. 4. Methods used to obtain loop closure. 5. A description of the process for implementing corrective action, to include both operational and personnel issues. 6. Confidentiality issues. VII. Field Operations Critical Care Transport units may be used to respond to 911 calls, at the discretion of the counties ALS contracted provider when staffed with an accredited paramedic and an EMT. When responding to 911 calls, the ALS crew will utilize Merced County ALS protocols.

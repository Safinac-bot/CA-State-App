## 700 Series – Adult Treatment Protocols

### 740 – Dystonic Reactions

- **Definition:** Phenothiazines and non-phenothiazines (e.g., Haloperidol) may cause dystonic reactions (restlessness, muscle spasms, oculogyric crisis, etc.).
- **BLS:** Oxygen (SpO₂ ≥94%), airway, vitals, glucose.
- **ALS:** Monitor rhythm, IV/IO access, Diphenhydramine 25mg IV/IO or IM, repeat once after 5 min if no response.


### 742 – Behavioral Emergency

- **Definition:** Prioritize responder/patient safety, request law enforcement for aggressive/violent patients. Preserve dignity, rights, and wellbeing.
- **BLS:** Scene safety, apply restraints as needed (monitor every 15 min), oxygen, vitals, glucose.
- **ALS:** IV/IO, Midazolam (2mg IV/IO, titrate up to 5mg max, or 5mg IN/IM), repeat after 10 min if required.


### 744 – Envenomation/Bites/Stings

- **Definition:** Local/systemic reactions; special sections for insects, black widow, snakebites.
- **BLS:** Remove stinger, cool for pain/swelling, oxygen, vitals, glucose, temperature, NPO, mark wound/swelling, immobilize extremities.
- **ALS:** Monitor, IV/IO, fluid for hypotension, pain management, black widow spasms: Midazolam up to 5 mg.


### 746 – Organophosphates Exposure

- **Definition:** SLUDGEM/ABSLUDGEM symptoms. Bronchospasm, secretions, bradycardia, etc.
- **BLS:** Decontaminate before care. Oxygen, vitals, glucose.
- **ALS:** Monitor, IV/IO, Atropine 2 mg IV/IO (repeat q5min until secretions dry), Ondansetron 4 mg, Midazolam up to 5 mg for seizures.


### 748 – OB-GYN Emergencies

- **Definition:** Field delivery, eclampsia, abnormal presentations, perinatal care.
- **BLS:** Oxygen, comfort, left lateral if distress, vitals, BP.
- **ALS:** Monitor, large bore IV, routine/blast delivery procedures, eclampsia: Midazolam 2-5 mg, magnesium for seizures.
- **Abnormals:** Prolapsed cord, breech, limb presentations—detailed actions provided.


### 750 – Spinal Motion Restriction

- **Definition:** Indications for SMR (trauma, pain, neuro, etc.), reduce unnecessary immobilization.
- **BLS/ALS:** Assessment, documentation, manual stabilization, selection of SMR/immobilization, special considerations by age/mechanism.


### 752 – Trauma

- **Definition:** Severe trauma management, triage, destination rules.
- **BLS:** Oxygen, vitals, SMR, bleeding control, splint, temperature, transport.
- **ALS:** Needle decompression, dual IV/IO, limited fluids, capnography, pain control.
- **Specifics:** Amputation, evisceration, flail chest, impaled objects, chest wounds.


### 756 – Burns

- **Definition:** Care for heat, cold, electricity, radiation, chemical burns.
- **BLS:** Scene safety, cooling, remove clothing/jewelry, oxygen, glucose, temperature, dressings.
- **ALS:** Airway as needed, dual IV/IO (severe), Parkland formula: [kg x TBSA%]/4 = ml/hr, pain management, albuterol for wheezing.


### 758 – TXA / Bleeding Control

- **Definition:** Admin of Tranexamic Acid (TXA) for trauma with risk of major hemorrhage.
- **Indications/Exclusions:** Age, time, BP, HR, exclusion criteria.
- **BLS:** Oxygen, vitals, bleeding control.
- **ALS:** Monitor, dual IV, capnography, prepare/administer TXA 1000 mg in 250 mL NS over 10 min (4gtt/sec), document and inform receiving.


### 760 – Airway Management

- **Definition:** ETT/IGel indications, contraindications, and training standards.
- **BLS:** Oxygen, position, BVM.
- **ALS:** ETT for ≥15yo as indicated (cardiac/resp arrest, GCS≤8), Bougie required with ETT, confirm with EtCO₂, two attempts per patient. iGel/SAD for BVM failure or difficult airway, with proper confirmation.

## 800 Series – Pediatric Treatment Protocols

### 802 – Cardiac Arrest

- **BLS:** CPR, AED, cycles per AHA PALS.
- **ALS:** Monitor, EtCO₂, asystole/PEA vs. VT/VF, Epi (0.01 mg/kg), Amio/Lido for VT/VF, defibrillation by weight, airway strategy, reversible causes, on-scene resuscitation timeframe, termination criteria.


### 806 – Bradycardia

- **BLS:** CPR <60 HR + severe symptoms, oxygen, glucose.
- **ALS:** Monitor, dual IV/IO, fluids, Epi (0.01 mg/kg), Atropine (0.02 mg/kg if refractory), pacing if ordered.


### 808 – Tachycardia

- **Definition:** Rates by age, signs of extremis, distinguish sinus vs. SVT/VT.
- **BLS:** Oxygen, glucose, vitals.
- **ALS:** Monitor, fluids (weight-based), capnography, cardioversion if unstable (1–2J/kg), Adenosine by base order.


### 812 – Airway Obstruction

- **Partial:** Monitor, avoid oral airway with infection/stridor.
- **Severe:** Back blows/chest thrusts (infant); abdominal thrusts/chest compressions if >1yo.
- **ALS:** Magill’s forceps/removal if unresponsive, needle cricothryotomy as detailed, neb Epi for stridor.


### 814 – Respiratory Distress

- **Asthma/Bronchiolitis/Croup/Epiglottitis:** BLS/ALS stratified by presentation.
- **Therapy:** Oxygen, CPAP ≥8yo (contraindications listed), albuterol, Epi for severe or croup, support, and minimization of agitation (epiglottitis).


### 816 – Nausea \& Vomiting

- **Indications:** Gastroenteritis, pain med side effect, etc.
- **BLS:** Oxygen, airway, glucose as needed.
- **ALS:** Fluids for severe dehydration, Ondansetron 2–4 mg (ODT/IV/IM, max 12 mg), Diphenhydramine if allergic to Ondansetron.


### 818 – Pain Management

- **Definition:** Any significant pain (trauma, sickle cell, etc.).
- **BLS:** Splinting, ice, elevation, psychological support.
- **ALS:** Acetaminophen, Ketorolac, Morphine, Fentanyl, all by weight, with documentation and special considerations, capnography for high-dose narcotics.


### 824 – Altered Level of Consciousness (ALOC) or Syncope

- **BLS:** Oxygen, glucose (oral if safe and low), Naloxone IN.
- **ALS:** Monitor, IV fluids, Dextrose/Glucagon per weight if hypoglycemia, Naloxone escalate to IV/IM as needed.


### 826 – Allergic Reaction \& Anaphylaxis Shock

- **BLS:** Remove allergen, oxygen, assist with patient’s meds, oral Diphenhydramine, Epi Jr or equivalent IM for anaphylaxis.
- **ALS:** Diphenhydramine IV/IM, Epi IM (repeat listed), Albuterol neb, fluid bolus if hypotensive, IV Epi (base order) if severely ill.


### 828 – Seizures

- **BLS:** Oxygen, position (left side), cool for febrile, glucose.
- **ALS:** Monitor, IV/IO TKO, Midazolam (0.1 mg/kg IV/IO or 0.2 mg/kg IN/IM, repeat once at 10 min).


### 830 – Hyperglycemia

- **BLS:** Oxygen, glucose check.
- **ALS:** Monitor, EtCO₂ if BG≥200, IV/IO fluids 20 ml/kg if acidotic (EtCO₂≤25).


### 832 – Hyperthermia

- **BLS:** Remove from heat, cooling, glucose, fluids PO if exhaustion.
- **ALS:** IV/IO fluids, Midazolam for seizures if present, by weight.


### 834 – Hypothermia

- **BLS:** Gentle warming, oxygen, glucose, CPR if needed, temperature algorithm.
- **ALS:** Monitor/limit defibrillation, warm IV fluids, Dextrose/Glucagon for hypoglycemia per weight.


### 836 – Medication Poisonings

- **BLS:** Oxygen, vitals, glucose.
- **ALS:** Fluids as indicated, sodium bicarb for tricyclic toxicity, midazolam for seizures, push-dose epi for hypotension (mixing instructions included).


### 838 – Non-Traumatic Shock

- **BLS:** Oxygen, glucose, temperature.
- **ALS:** Capnography, fluids 20 ml/kg bolus (max 40 ml/kg), push-dose epi for refractory hypotension (mixing instructions), address reversible causes.


### 840 – Dystonic Reactions

- **BLS/ALS:** Oxygen, glucose, Diphenhydramine 1 mg/kg IV/IO/IM (max 25 mg, repeat once at 5 minutes).


### 844 – Envenomation/Bites/Stings

- **BLS:** Remove stingers, ice pack, wound marking, immobilize, fluids, airway/oxygen.
- **ALS:** Monitor, fluids, pain, severe spasms: Midazolam 0.1 mg/kg (max 1 mg, repeat once), assess for anaphylaxis.


### 846 – Organophosphates Exposure

- **BLS:** Decontamination, oxygen, glucose, vitals.
- **ALS:** Atropine 0.05 mg/kg q5min to dry secretions, Ondansetron, Midazolam for seizures, refer to Nerve Agent Protocol.


### 848 – Newborn Resuscitation

- **BLS/ALS:** APGAR, temperature, cord care, resuscitation based on heart rate strata (HR>100, HR 80-100, HR 60-80, HR<60), airway support, compressions, epinephrine IV/IO if HR<80.


### 852 – Trauma

- **BLS:** Standard trauma care, SMR, bleeding control, splint/temperature maintenance, expeditious transport.
- **ALS:** Tension pneumothorax decompression, fluids by weight if hypotensive, capnography, pain management, specific injuries (amputation, impaled, flailed chest) per policy.
- **Triage criteria** include GCS, SBP, abnormal RR, anatomic injuries, <1yo with fracture, EMS judgment.


### 854 – Traumatic Arrest

- **BLS:** CPR, airway (simple adjunct/BVM), bleeding control, SMR.
- **ALS:** Monitor, bilateral needle thoracostomy, fluids, cardiac meds per arrest protocol, determination of death protocol detailed.


### 856 – Burns

- **BLS:** Scene safety, copious water for thermal/chemical/powder burns, airway, glucose, temperature management, dressing choice by TBSA.
- **ALS:** Advanced airway, dual IV for severe, Parkland formula FLR, albuterol, document rule of nines.


### 860 – Airway Management

- **Definition:** Supraglottic Airway Device (SAD/i-gel) for peds, no ETT by paramedic scope.
- **Indications/Contraindications:** Cardiac arrest, BVM failure, trauma, size/age specs.
- **Emphasis on BLS airway support and aggressive intervention; complete placement and confirmation steps provided. Only FP-C/CCP-C/CFRN may intubate peds.

This includes A) Reduction of gross motor movement by the patient, B) Prevention of duplicating the damaging mechanism to the spine, and C) Regular reassessment of the motor, vascular, and sensory functions. This protocol has not been composed to “clear” patients who may or may not have spinal injuries in the field. This policy seeks to provide an evidence-based approach (whenever possible) that directs the careful practice of Spinal Motion Restriction through set criteria. This may include modified positioning for patients that have experienced traumatic injuries. Place these patients in Spinal Motion Restriction when the benefits outweigh the risks. When the benefits do not outweigh the risks, patients should not incur clinically unnecessary immobilization with hard collars and back boards based on tradition alone. Symptomatic tachycardia is defined by the following:
• Heart rate above:  
  o 220 - Infants (less than 2 years of age)  
  o 180 - Toddlers (ages 2 years and greater)  
• Systolic Blood Pressure - Hypotensive verified by length-based assessment tape or application  
• Associated signs and symptoms including ALOC, signs of shock, chest pain, acute pulmonary edema, syncope, extreme weakness  
• ECG rhythms will have no P-waves (both wide and narrow QRS)
• Do not confuse Sinus Tachycardia with supraventricular tachycardia (SVT). If a patient has Sinus Tachycardia look for signs of dehydration, sepsis, or shock and correct the cause.

OXYGEN  
As appropriate, goal is to maintain SpO2 at least 94%, secure airway and assist ventilations as necessary.  
VITALS

BLOOD SUGAR

CHECK

MONITOR  
Treat arrhythmias as appropriate.

If patient is hypotensive, establish two (2) large bore IV/IO’s and administer 20 ml/kg bolus. Reassess patient after bolus. Repeat if necessary at 10 ml/kg bolus up to a total of 40 ml/kg max has been administered.

# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 846 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS - ORGANOPHOSPHATES EXPOSURE Effective Date 10/2024 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 11/2018 Review Date: 10/2026 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. DEFINITION Organophosphate poisonings may cause bronchospasm, an increase in pulmonary and nasal secretions, constricted pupils, vomiting, diarrhea, urinary incontinence, diaphoresis, and cardiac dysrhythmias including both bradycardia and AV blocks. Remember the following mnemonic: SLUDGEM (Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Defecation, Gastric upset, Emesis and Miosis.) II. BLS TREATMENT Ensure patient has been decontaminated before providing care. Refer to EMS Policy \#915 - Hazardous Materials Incidents. OXYGEN As appropriate, goal to maintain SpO2 at least 94%. Assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vital signs. BLOOD SUGAR Test blood glucose and treat as appropriate. III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS Establish IV/IO and administer rate as appropriate. ATROPINE Administer 0.05mg/kg IV/IO repeat every 5 minutes as necessary to dry the secretions. ONDANSETRON Administer 2-4 mg ODT, IV/IO, IM. Try to administer ODT as first route of choice if possible; may administer every 10 minutes as necessary to max dose of 12 mg total. MIDAZOLAM Administer 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO/IM/IN push. MAX dose of 1 mg for seizure control; may repeat once after 10 minutes if seizures continue. Refer to length-based assessment tape or application for drug doses and proper equipment sizes.

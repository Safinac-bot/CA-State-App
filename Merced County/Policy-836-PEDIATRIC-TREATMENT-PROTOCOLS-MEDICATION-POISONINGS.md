# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 836 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS – MEDICATION POISONINGS Effective Date 01/2024 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 11/2018 Review Date: 01/2026 PAGE 1 of 2 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director S T R I V I N G F O R E X C E L L E N C E I. DEFINITION Known or suspected drug overdose, poisoning, or accidental ingestion. Common symptoms include weakness/dizziness, mild confusion, syncope, unconsciousness, bradycardia, hypotension, arrhythmias/widening QRS and seizures. Beta Blockers – Metoprolol/Lopressor/Troprol, Labetalol/Esmolol, Carvedilol/Coreg, Atenolol/Tenormin (amongst others) Calcium Channel Blockers – Norvasc/Amlodipine, Diltiazem/Cardizem, Nifedipine/Procardia, Verapamil/Calan (amongst others) Tricyclic Antidepressants – Amitriptyline, Doxepin, Imipramine, Nortriptyline, Trimipramine (amongst others) II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate, goal is to maintain SpO₂ at least 94%, secure airway and assist ventilations as necessary. VITALS Assess vitals. BLOOD SUGAR Test glucose and treat as appropriate. CHECK III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS If patient is hypotensive, according to length-based assessment tape or application; establish 2 large bore IV/IO’s and administer 20 ml/kg bolus. Reassess patient after bolus. Repeat as necessary at 10 ml/kg bolus up to MAX OF 40 ml/kg has been administered. Subject: PEDIATRIC TREATMENT PROTOCOLS – MEDICATION POISONINGS Policy Number 836 Page 2 of 2 S T R I V I N G F O R E X C E L L E N C E TRICYCLIC ANTIDEPRESSANT POISONINGS SODIUM Administer 1 mEq/kg slow IV/IO push for cardiac dysrhythmia or QRS BICARBONATE complex wider than 0.10 sec Repeat as necessary. Max total dose is 2 mEq/kg. MIDAZOLAM If the patient is in status seizures, administer 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO (max dose 5 mg) or 0.2 mg/kg IN/IM (max dose 5 mg) if no immediate IV/IO access. PUSH DOSE Administer 10 mcg (1 ml) slow IV push every 1-5 minutes for hypotension and EPINEHRINE patient not responding to previous treatment. PUSH DOSE EPINEPHRINE SOLUTION MIXING INSTRUCTIONS • Take Epinephrine 1:10,000 concentration (1 mg/10 ml) and waste 9 ml of Epinephrine • In same syringe draw 9 ml of saline from the patient’s IV bag and shake well • Mixture now provides 10 ml of Epinephrine at 10mcg/ml (0.01 mg/ml) concentration • Label syringe Epinephrine 10 mcg/ml Refer to length-based assessment tape or application for drug doses and proper equipment sizes.

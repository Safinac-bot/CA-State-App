# EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES AGENCY POLICY NUMBER 728 A Division of the Merced County Department of Public Health SUBJECT: ADULT TREATMENT PROTOCOLS – SEIZURES Effective Date 07/2024 Authority: Health and Safety Code, Division 2.5, and California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 9, Chapter 4, 1797.220 Initial Date: 10/2011 Review Date: 07/2026 PAGE 1 of 1 Signatures on File Approved By: Tim Williams Ajinder Singh, MD EMS Administrator EMS Medical Director STRIVING FOR EXCELLENCE I. DEFINITION All seizures from any cause (epilepsy, hypoglycemia, infection, intoxication, poisoning, or eclampsia) are managed the same. Remember to provide supportive care and to check for life threatening causes. Reassess the patient and watch for recurrent seizure activity. Status Seizures – Any actively seizing patient who has been seizing for more than 10 minutes, or an actively seizing patient with recurrent seizures and no reawakening in between seizures. II. BLS TREATMENT OXYGEN As appropriate, goal is to maintain SpO2 at least 94%, secure airway and assist ventilations as necessary. POSITION If not contraindicated by injuries, place patient in left lateral decubitus position. VITALS Assess vital signs, monitor airway and ventilatory effort and assist ventilations as needed. BLOOD SUGAR Test glucose and treat as appropriate if blood sugar is < 60 mg/dl. Refer to CHECK EMS Policy \#724 Adult Treatment Protocols – Altered Level of Consciences (ALOC) or Syncope III. ALS TREATMENT MONITOR Treat rhythm as appropriate. VASCULAR ACCESS IV/IO TKO. MIDAZOLAM Administer 2 mg IV/IO push. Titrate in 1 mg increments for seizure control, max dose 5 mg IV/IO. Administer 5 mg IN or IM if no immediate IV/IO access available. May repeat once after 10 minutes if seizures continue. MAGNESIUM For patients 24 weeks pregnant or more, administer 2 gms in 10 ml saline SULFATE slow IV push over 2 minutes.
